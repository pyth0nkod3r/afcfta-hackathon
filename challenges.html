Explore the Hackathon Challenges

Discover the core problems, innovative solution directions, and the technologies you can use to tackle each challenge at the AfCFTA Digital Trade Hackathon.

1. E-Commerce Facilitation & Regulation

Key Problem:

Fragmented digital trade policies, lack of harmonized consumer protection, and poor cross-border trust.

Solutions Sought:

Platforms or tools for policy compliance automation, digital trade transparency, cross-border digital contracts, and user protection.

Technology Examples:

Blockchain for smart contracts, AI for regulation tracking, APIs for compliance verification.

2. Internet and Last-Mile Connectivity

Key Problem:

Rural and underserved areas lack reliable and affordable internet access, limiting digital participation.

Solutions Sought:

Community network solutions, low-cost connectivity hardware, mesh networks, or data-sharing platforms.

Technology Examples:

IoT mesh networks, satellite internet, low-Earth orbit (LEO) technology, solar-powered routers.

3. Digital Payments & Financial Inclusion

Key Problem:

Many Africans remain unbanked or underbanked, and digital payment systems are not always interoperable.

Solutions Sought:

Inclusive fintech apps, interoperable cross-border mobile money platforms, micro-lending, and savings tools.

Technology Examples:

Blockchain wallets, USSD-based solutions, API banking, AI-driven credit scoring.

4. Logistics Infrastructure Expansion

Key Problem:

Inefficient logistics and delivery systems hinder e-commerce scalability and regional trade.

Solutions Sought:

Smart logistics platforms, cross-border tracking systems, intelligent warehousing, and crowd-sourced delivery models.

Technology Examples:

GPS tracking systems, RFID tech, AI for route optimization, digital logistics dashboards.

5. Youth and MSMEs Participation & Empowerment

Key Problem:

Limited access to digital tools, mentorship, and funding for young people and small businesses.

Solutions Sought:

Platforms for MSME onboarding, mentorship hubs, youth-focused digital skilling and funding marketplaces.

Technology Examples:

LMS (Learning Management Systems), mobile apps, AI mentorship bots, community forums.

6. E-Commerce Adoption & Demand

Key Problem:

Low consumer trust and engagement with online marketplaces across Africa.

Solutions Sought:

Easy-to-use storefront platforms, secure checkout systems, gamified loyalty programs, localized content.

Technology Examples:

Progressive Web Apps (PWAs), AI chatbots, mobile-first UI/UX, secure payment gateways.

7. Africa and Global Market Penetration

Key Problem:

African businesses face barriers in reaching regional and international markets.

Solutions Sought:

Digital export tools, market intelligence dashboards, e-trade certification platforms, virtual trade shows.

Technology Examples:

Data visualization tools, blockchain for provenance tracking, SaaS export platforms.
