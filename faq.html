Have Questions About the Hackathon?
Find Answers Here!

Get detailed information about the AFCFTA Digital Trade Protocol Hackathon, from registration and eligibility to prizes and technical requirements. If you can't find your answer here, feel free to contact our support team.

The AFCFTA Digital Trade Protocol Hackathon is a 5-day event during the Science of Trade Conference 2025, bringing together developers, policymakers, startups, and industry experts to develop innovative solutions for implementing the AFCFTA Digital Trade Protocol. The event focuses on digitalizing trade processes, enhancing regulatory infrastructure, and improving logistics systems across Africa.

The winning team will receive N20 million in prize money and mentorship opportunities. Additional awards include Best Overall Solution, Most Innovative Solution, Best Regulatory Compliance, and Best Logistics and Delivery Solution. Winners will also have opportunities to pilot their solutions with regional trade organizations.

Each team consists of 5 members with diverse skills: 2 Developers/Programmers, 1 UI/UX Designer, 1 Trade Expert, and 1 Business Strategist. There will be 10 teams in total, selected based on expertise in technology, trade, logistics, and business strategy.

The hackathon focuses on four key areas: Digital trade facilitation tools, Regulatory infrastructure for e-documentation, Trust services and digital identities, and Logistics and last-mile delivery optimization across Africa.

Participants receive ongoing guidance from experts in trade, technology, and logistics industries. The event includes workshops, mentorship sessions, and access to resources needed for development. Winners receive additional support through incubation programs and opportunities to implement their solutions.

Applications open on February 1, 2025, through our official hackathon platform. Candidates will be evaluated based on their expertise in technology, trade, logistics, or business strategy. Selected participants will be notified by March 18-22, 2025.
